Seven Lakes

The lakes are maars or low-profile volcanic craters located in the San Pablo Volcanic Field. They are among the 200 small monogenetic volcanoes found along the Macolod Corridor, a rift zone between Mount Makiling and Mount Banahaw.

Entrance Fee in Mohicap - P10
Bamboo Rafting in Pandin with Lunch - P360

Bato Springs Resort

Tucked in the city of San Pablo, Laguna, it is frequently visited by weekend warriors in search of a relaxing environment. The place is relaxing, surrounded by lots of trees and at the middle is stream like pool cascading on walls of rocks.

Entrance Fee (For both kids and adults)
Weekends / Holidays: PHP 100
Weekdays: PHP 80
Overnight: PHP 150

Pagsanjan Falls

Pagsanjan Falls is one of the most famous waterfalls in the Philippines. Located in the province of Laguna, the falls is one of the major tourist attractions of the region.

Entrance fee: Seeing the waterfall is FREE, but if you want to access other areas of Pagsanjan park proper, there’s a fee ranging from PHP 20 – PHP 300
Shooting the rapids in Pagsanjan Falls: PHP 1,250/person (rate includes round-trip boat ride, entrance fees, and use of life vests)

Dampalit Falls

The falls of Dampalit is tall and falls into a relatively shallow catch basin that continues as a series of much shorter albeit wider drops into a maze of huge rocks that dot the stream. The lush, green surroundings make the place an ideal picnic area.

Entrance Fee: PHP 20 per person
Rent huts and tables: PHP 200-300

Enchanted Kingdom

`

Enchanted Kingdom is a theme park in the Philippines. It is located in Santa Rosa, Laguna. It has a land area of 25 hectares. The park is managed and operated by Enchanted Kingdom Inc. Enchanted Kingdom is a member of the International Association of Amusement Parks and Attractions.

Regular Day Pass: PHP 800 (Unlimited park rides)

Nuvali Park

NUVALI is the country’s first and largest eco-city development, built on the principles of sustainable design. NUVALI offers nature amenities such as its 35-kilometer mountain bike trail, a multi-functional lake, hiking paths, a wildlife and bird sanctuary, and Camp N teambuilding and camping grounds.

No Entrance Fee but there are outdoor activities that you can join such as wakeboarding that 350 for an hour

Rizal Shrine

The Rizal Shrine in Calamba (Museo ni José Rizal Calamba) is a reproduction of the original two-story, Spanish-Colonial style house in Calamba, Laguna where José Rizal was born on June 19, 1861.

Entrance fee: Free but donations are accepted

Hidden Valley Springs

A truly tropical rainforest paradise. The nearest town to Hidden Vally Laguna is Calauan and the springs are accessed down a winding road, away from the traffic, nestled in a charming, rural setting.

Admission Fees - PHP 2500 (includes lunch buffet and afternoon snack)

Mt. Makiling

An inactive stratovolcano located in the provinces of Laguna and Batangas on the island of Luzon, Philippines. The mountain rises to an elevation of 1,090 m (3,580 ft) above mean sea level and is the highest feature of the Laguna Volcanic Field. Mount Makiling is a state-owned forest reserve administered by the University of the Philippines Los Baños.

Entrance Fee: PHP 40
Camping Fee: PHP 200 (for a group of five) They have a “No ID, No Entry” policy, so be sure to bring one during your visit

Hulugan Falls

Massive waterfall surrounded by thick forest with a hiking trail & swimming area. It is also quite wide, maybe 20-25 metres (60-70 feet), and has a strong flow of water.

Registration fee – PHP 30 per person
Guide fee – PHP 500 for Hulugan Falls only; PHP 600 for the 3 waterfalls (good for 5 people)

Nagcarlan Underground Cemetery

The Nagcarlan Underground Cemetery (Libingan sa Ilalim ng Lupa ng Nagcarlan) is a national historical landmark and museum in Barangay Bambang, Nagcarlan, Laguna supervised by the National Historical Commission of the Philippines.

Entrance fee: Free but donations are accepted

Pila Heritage Site

Pila’s town plaza, including the nearby ancestral houses, was declared a National Historical Landmark last 2000 by the National Historical Institute of the Philippines. The town plaza is just a short drive away from the national highway.

Entrance fee: Free but donations are accepted

Japanese Garden

Located near Caliraya Lake between Lumban and Cavinti, the garden was developed to honor the soldiers who died during the Second World War. You will be greeted upon entrance with a large torii, reminding you that you are about to enter a quiet, hallowed ground.

Entrance fee: Free

Makiling Botanical Garden

The Makiling Botanical Garden was built in September 1965 to support professional instruction and research related to forestry and plant sciences. Makiling botanic gardens offers a safe and beautiful hike through the forest of Mount Maquiling. The longer trails offer a challenging hike with many ups and downs and stream beds. The trees are old and full of epiphytes.

Entrance fee: PHP 40

Republic Waterpark

The Newest and Most Innovative Wakepark in the World! Republ1c Wakepark, a place for wakeboarding in Laguna and other water sports enthusiasts Republic Wakepark is the best place to learn wakeboarding because they only provide professionals to teach all the right techniques while having fun.

Entrance fee: PHP 250

Sulyap Gallery Cafe

Experience a sumptuous “taste of the past” with multi-awarded and world-renowned Sulyap Gallery Café, Boutique Hotels and Restaurant. They offer the best traditional and modern Filipino Cuisine, the comfort of a bed and breakfast scene, and the grand experience of exploring our classical picturesque halls.

Budget: PHP 500 – 3000
